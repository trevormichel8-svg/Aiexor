Pricing AI Domains

How to Price an AI Domain Name Fairly

A simple framework founders and investors can use to price AI domain names without guesswork.

Why pricing AI domains is different from normal domains

AI domains sit in a fast-moving market. New products launch weekly, funding rounds push up demand, and suddenly a short, brandable name becomes the missing piece in a rebrand or new product line. That means pricing needs to consider more than just length and TLD.

Key factors that influence AI domain pricing

  • Brand strength: Does the name feel like it could be a company, not just a project?
  • Length and structure: 5–8 character, pronounceable .com names are often worth a premium.
  • Market fit: Does the name align well with AI, automation, analytics, or agents?
  • Comparable sales: Past sales of similar AI brands give a pricing baseline.
  • Buyer profile: A well-funded SaaS startup can afford more than a solo developer side project.

Example: why a name like Aiexor.com justifies a premium

Aiexor.com combines a strong AI signal (“AI”) with a clean, brandable ending (“exor”) that feels modern but not trendy. It’s short, visually balanced, and easy to pronounce in multiple languages — all traits that brand strategists and naming agencies look for.

For that reason, a mid four-figure price range is reasonable for a name that can front a full AI platform, not just a small tool. The goal is to price high enough to reflect its potential, but realistically enough to move when the right founder discovers it.

A simple pricing framework

  • Start from comparable AI domain sales in marketplaces and reports.
  • Adjust based on length, clarity, and potential use cases.
  • Set a public “Buy Now” price and a private floor for negotiation.
  • Leave room for payment plans or structured deals for the right buyer.
